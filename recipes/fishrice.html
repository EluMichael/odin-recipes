Fish Rice Recipe

Description

Fish Rice is a versatile, nutrient-dense, and quick-cooking comfort meal consisting of fried or baked fish tossed with rice and crunchy vegetables.

Ingredients

  • Rice: Use any kind of non-starchy rice because it is less sticky, like basmati or jasmine, but any type of long-grain rice will work.
  • Fish: Use 2 to 3 fillets or steaks of any firm fish you like, such as salmon, snapper, Spanish mackerel (kingfish), cod, bass, haddock, or catfish.
  • Ghee/butter/oil: Use ghee for flavor, or a mix of ghee and oil.
  • Alternatively, use unsalted high-quality butter or your preferred cooking oil.
  • Aromatics: 1 small onion (thinly sliced), 1 teaspoon chopped garlic, 3/4 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste (or 1/2 tsp each grated), and 2 sprigs of curry leaves (divided).
  • Spices: 1/2 to 1 teaspoon red chili powder, 1 teaspoon garam masala, 1/8 teaspoon turmeric powder (optional), 1/4 to 1/2 teaspoon black pepper, and salt.
  • Vegetables: 1 1/2 cups chopped carrots, 1 cup frozen peas (thawed), and 2 to 3 green chilies (slit).
  • Extras: 10 whole cashews (split), 10 raisins (optional), and 1 to 1 1/2 tablespoons lemon juice.

Steps

  1. Cook Rice: Add 1 cup of uncooked rice to a bowl and rinse well.
  2. Drain completely, pour in fresh water, and bring to a rolling boil.
  3. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and cook until the water is absorbed and the rice is cooked through.
  4. Prep the Fish: Coat the fish with lemon juice, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, and salt.
  5. Leave aside for 10 minutes while you prep the onions and other ingredients.
  6. Fry the Fish: Heat oil or ghee and add half of the curry leaves.
  7. Place the fish in the pan and fry for 3 minutes, then flip.
  8. Add 1/3 of the red chili powder and fry on both sides until cooked through. Transfer to a plate.
  9. Make Fish Rice: Heat ghee or oil in the same pan.
  10. Add garlic, onions, carrots, green chilies, cashews, raisins, and the remaining curry leaves.
  11. Saute until the nuts turn lightly golden and the onions begin to caramelize, taking care not to burn them.
  12. Add the cooled rice, salt, peas, and pepper.
  13. Mix well and heat through; taste and adjust salt or pepper as needed.
  14. Serve the rice and fish together.
