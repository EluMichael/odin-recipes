Pounded Yam with Egusi Soup Recipe

Description

Nigerian egusi soup, also known as efo elegusi, is a delicious and versatile soup served with pounded yam, which is a staple dish.

Ingredients

  • Pounded Yam: 1 medium yam, water, and salt.
  • Egusi Soup Base: 1 1/2 cups egusi, 1/4 cup palm oil, and 1 tablespoon fermented locust beans.
  • Proteins: 3 tablespoons ground crayfish, 1 cup dried fish, 3 smoked mackerel fillets, and 1 cup mixed seafood.

Steps

Pounded Yam

  1. Peel the yam, slice, and wash it.
  2. Cook with water and salt until completely soft.
  3. Using a mortar and pestle, pound the yam until soft and sticky, sprinkling with yam water at intervals.
  4. Mold into desired shapes and set aside.

Egusi Soup

  1. Blend the red bell pepper, Scotch bonnet peppers, tomatoes, and one onion in a food processor with a cup of water.
  2. Defrost the frozen seafood, then wash and slice the kale.
  3. Chop the remaining onion and cut the smoked mackerel fillets into smaller pieces.
  4. Heat the oil in a saucepan for 30 seconds, then add the chopped onions.
  5. Fry until the onions appear translucent, then add the locust beans and stir for one minute.
  6. Add the blended pepper mix and cook for about 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Add 3 to 4 cups of water, the dried fish, ground crayfish, and a little salt to taste.
  8. Note: Smoked mackerel is salty and will increase the soup's saltiness, so adjust seasoning accordingly.
  9. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  10. Mix 2 tablespoons of the cooking sauce into the egusi to make a thick paste.
  11. Form small egusi balls by hand and drop them into the pan.
  12. Reduce the heat and cook for at least 20 minutes.
  13. Mix in the kale and smoked mackerel.
  14. If the soup is too thick, add more water and simmer for 10 minutes on low heat.
  15. Add the seafood and cook for 5 minutes.
  16. Remove from heat and serve.
